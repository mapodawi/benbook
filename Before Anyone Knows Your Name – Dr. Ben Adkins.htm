Let me tell you a quick story.

In 2010, I was a chiropractor with a brand new practice, a very empty waiting room, and about $200 left in my marketing budget after printing business cards that nobody was picking up from the sandwich shop down the street.

I couldn't afford a billboard. I couldn't afford a radio ad. I definitely couldn't afford to hire a marketing agency.

So I did the only thing I could afford. I started messing around on the internet.

I taught myself how to build a basic website. I figured out how to get my practice showing up in Google searches. I started posting on social media before most local businesses even had a Facebook page.

Most of what I tried didn't work. But some of it did. And the stuff that worked… worked fast.

Within a few months, other business owners started noticing. The restaurant owner across the street asked how I was getting so many Google reviews. A lawyer friend asked if I could "do whatever you're doing" for his firm.

I said yes to a few of them. Then a few more. Before I knew it, I was running a digital marketing agency on the side of my chiropractic practice.

"By 2013, I sold my chiropractic practice and went full-time into the agency. Since then, the businesses I've built have generated millions of dollars - for me and for the clients I've served along the way."

This book is every strategy I used to get there. And every strategy I've learned, borrowed, tested, and refined in the years since.

I wrote it for you - the service business owner (or soon-to-be business owner) who has the skills to deliver great work but hasn't figured out how to get people through the door yet. The one sitting where I sat in 2010.

Section 1: Borrowed Credibility

You don't have a reputation yet - so you borrow one. Attach yourself to charities, chambers of commerce, business associations, and well-connected people your prospects already trust. Includes cause-based leverage, platform leverage, institutional leverage, data and analysis leverage, community and relationship leverage, and content and education leverage.

When a prospect has no reason to trust you, give them no reason to say no. Structure your offers so the client pays nothing if you don't deliver. Financial risk reversal, speed and availability as trust - models that make your lack of track record completely irrelevant.
